Happiness Around the World

IN 2020, Finland is on top of the list of the HAPPIEST country in the world, for the THIRD consecutive year, followed by Denmark and Switzerland.

On the other end of the spectrum, Afghanistan, being an UNDERDEVELOPED country, often experiencing WAR, POLITICAL INSTABILITY and natural CATASTROPHES, was labelled unhappiest place in the world.

Based on the box plots for the year 2015 - 2020, it is seen that the average Happiness level in 2020 has increased slightly compared to the past four years.

However, the minimum value of the box plot for 2020 is SMALLER than all the previous years' minimum value. Hence, it can be concluded that the happiness level in "unhappy countries" is DECREASING EVERY YEAR.

Factors Affecting Happiness?

The scatter plot matrix above shows the correlations between happiness level, gdp per capita and social support. According to statistics, gdp per capita and social support are the two MAIN FACTORS that affect happiness level in a country. From the scatter plot matrix, the data points for gdp per capita and happiness score are the most tightly clustered along an imaginary line.

The scatter plot shows how gdp per capita affects life expectancy in different regions of the world. The size of the bubbles is also indicative of the happiness level in that region.

It is clearly seen that there is a POSITIVE CORRELATION between gdp per capita and life expectancy. Also, Western European countries having the highest gdp per capita and higher life expectancy, are also found to be the HAPPIEST people.
